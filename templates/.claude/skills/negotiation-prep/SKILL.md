# /negotiation-prep

Prepares you for any negotiation or high-stakes conversation — salary, contract, pricing,
scope change, partnership, or difficult internal discussion.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Before producing any output, use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. What is this negotiation about? (e.g., salary, contract renewal, vendor pricing, scope)
2. What is your ideal outcome? (be specific — a number, a term, an agreement)
3. What is your walk-away position? (the minimum you'll accept before you leave)
4. Who is on the other side and what do they want?
5. What is your BATNA — your best alternative if this negotiation fails?
6. Any history or relationship context I should know?

Do not produce any output until the user answers all questions.

## Step 2 — Produce the Prep Package

Output these five sections in order:

### My Opening Position
2-3 sentences the user could say verbatim to open the negotiation.
Clear. Non-defensive. States what they want without apologizing for it.
Does not reveal the walk-away position.

### Their Likely Position
What the other side wants and the interests (not just positions) driving it.
Include: their constraints, pressures, and what a win looks like for them.
This section should feel like genuine empathy, not a tactical analysis.

### Common Ground
Where the two sides actually agree.
Negotiations start better from shared interests than from the gap.
At least 2 items.

### My 3 Moves

| Move | What I Say | What I'm Willing to Trade |
|---|---|---|
| Opening offer | [specific, slightly above ideal] | [what I'd give up for their full acceptance] |
| First concession | [step toward middle] | [smaller trade] |
| Final position | [minimum acceptable] | [walk away if rejected] |

### Objection Responses

| Their Objection | My Response |
|---|---|
| [Most likely objection] | [Direct, non-defensive response] |
| [Budget/timing objection] | [...] |
| [Comparison to competitor] | [...] |
| [Escalation to a decision-maker] | [...] |
| [Silence / delay tactic] | [...] |

Minimum 5 rows. More if the context calls for it.

## Step 3 — Roleplay Option

After delivering the prep package, ask:
"Do you want to roleplay the conversation? I'll play the other side — I'll push back realistically
based on what I know about their position."

If yes: start immediately. Play the other side faithfully, including their real objections.
Do not be artificially easy. The purpose is to surface surprises before they happen live.

After the roleplay: offer one paragraph of feedback on what went well and what to sharpen.
