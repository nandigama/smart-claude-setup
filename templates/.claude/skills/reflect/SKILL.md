# /reflect

A guided reflection session for moments when you need to think clearly — about a decision,
a difficult situation, your progress, or just what's going on beneath the surface.

Not a therapy session. Not a productivity audit. A structured conversation that produces
clarity where there was noise.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. What's on your mind? (describe freely — it can be a situation, a feeling, a decision, or "I don't know where to start")
2. What kind of reflection do you want?
   - **Clarity** — understand what I'm actually feeling or thinking
   - **Decision** — think through a choice I need to make
   - **Progress** — honest look at how things are going
   - **Debrief** — process something that happened (good or bad)
3. How much time do you have? (5 min / 15 min / open-ended)

Do not produce any output until the user answers.

## Step 2 — The Reflection

### Surface What's Underneath

Reflect back what the user shared — not a summary, but the subtext:
- What seems to be the real tension or concern?
- What is the user not quite saying directly?
- What assumption is embedded in how they framed it?

State this clearly: "What I'm hearing underneath is..."

### Ask the Useful Question

Depending on the mode:

**Clarity mode**: "What would you tell a close friend who was in this situation?"

**Decision mode**: "If you already knew the answer, what would it be? What's making you doubt it?"

**Progress mode**: "What would 'good enough' look like here? Are you measuring against the right thing?"

**Debrief mode**: "What do you want to take forward from this — and what do you want to leave behind?"

Give the user space to respond to that question before continuing.

### Synthesis

After the user responds, produce:

**What's clear**: the insight or understanding that emerged
**What's still open**: the question that's genuinely unresolved (not everything needs an answer today)
**One thing**: if there's one thing to do or think about differently, what is it?

Keep this short. Reflection is diminished by over-analysis.

## Step 3 — Capture or Continue

Ask: "Do you want to (a) capture this as a journal entry, (b) turn any open items into tasks, or (c) go deeper on anything specific?"

If journal entry: produce a 150-200 word first-person reflection in the user's voice, dated today.
If tasks: extract any concrete next actions from the reflection — specific, ownable, time-bound.
If deeper: pick the one unresolved thread and ask one more probing question.
