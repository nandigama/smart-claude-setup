# /page-cro

CRO analysis for a landing page, pricing page, sign-up flow, or any funnel step.
Identifies what's killing conversions and produces specific fixes — copy, layout, and flow.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. Paste the page content / URL / describe the page
2. What is the conversion goal? (sign-up / demo request / purchase / email capture / other)
3. What is the current conversion rate? (if known)
4. What is the traffic source? (paid search / organic / social / referral — matters for audience intent)
5. What does your customer believe before they land on this page? (their awareness level / what they already know)
6. Do you have any data on where people drop off? (heatmaps, session recordings, form completion data)

Do not produce any output until the user answers all questions.

## Step 2 — Produce the CRO Analysis

### Conversion Friction Audit

Evaluate across the five conversion killers:

**1. Unclear value proposition**
- Does the headline communicate what you do and for whom in one sentence?
- Is the benefit (outcome) clear before the feature (capability)?
- Would a first-time visitor know if this is for them within 5 seconds?

**2. Trust deficit**
- Social proof: testimonials / logos / case studies present?
- Are they specific and credible (vs. generic "great product!")?
- Is there a clear answer to "why should I trust you with my [email / money / data]?"

**3. CTA confusion**
- Is there one primary CTA or competing CTAs?
- Does the CTA say what happens next? ("Start free trial" > "Submit")
- Is the CTA above the fold? Is it repeated?

**4. Copy-to-buyer mismatch**
- Is the copy written for the buyer's problem or the company's features?
- Does it address the #1 objection the buyer has at this stage?
- Is it in the buyer's language or internal jargon?

**5. Friction in the conversion action**
- How many fields in the form? (each field reduces conversions ~5%)
- Is social login available?
- Is there unnecessary pre-commitment (pricing before value)?
