# /sales-engineer

Technical pre-sales workflows for SEs — from demo prep through POC design to closing the
technical win. Bridges product capability to the prospect's specific technical requirements.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. What do you need? (select one)
   - **Demo prep** — tailor a demo to a specific prospect
   - **POC scope** — design a proof of concept
   - **Technical objection** — handle a specific technical objection
   - **Solution architecture** — map your product to their technical environment
   - **RFI/RFP response** — answer technical questions in a formal process
2. Describe the prospect: company, industry, technical team size, stack
3. What problem are they trying to solve? (their stated problem and the real underlying one)
4. What is the competitive situation? (incumbent / competitor in the deal / greenfield)
5. Who is in the room? (technical evaluator / IT / security / CTO / mixed)
6. What is the deal stage and timeline?

Do not produce any output until the user answers all questions.

## Step 2 — Produce the SE Output

### Demo Prep

**Objective**: What is the one thing this demo must prove?

**Discovery you need before the demo**:
- [specific question to ask before walking in]
- [technical detail that changes the demo path]

**Demo flow** (30-45 min):
1. **Their world first** (5 min): frame the problem in their language before showing anything
2. **The "aha" moment** (10 min): the single feature/capability most relevant to their use case
3. **Technical depth** (15 min): the thing their technical evaluator will ask about
4. **Proof points** (5 min): customer story most similar to them
5. **Technical Q&A** (10 min)

**Lines to customize**: [specific places in the demo script to use their company name, their use case, their metrics]

**Common demo killers for this audience**: [what typically kills momentum in technical demos with this profile]

### POC Scope

```markdown
# POC Scope Document

## Objective
[What the POC will prove — one specific capability or integration]

## Success Criteria (defined upfront, agreed by both sides)
- [ ] [Measurable criterion 1]
- [ ] [Measurable criterion 2]

## Out of Scope
- [What the POC will NOT cover]

## Duration
[2-4 weeks recommended]

## What We Need From You
- [Technical access / sample data / stakeholder time]

## What We Provide
- [Environment / support / documentation]

## Go / No-Go Decision Point
[At the end of the POC, we both agree on these criteria to move forward]
```

### Technical Objection Handling

For the objection provided:

**What they're really asking**: [the fear or concern underneath the technical objection]
**Direct answer**: [the factual response]
**Proof**: [reference customer / documentation / demonstration]
**Reframe**: [how to turn this from a blocker into a differentiator]
**When to escalate**: [if they push further, who to bring in]

## Step 3 — Competitive Positioning

Ask: "Do you want a technical battle card against the specific competitor in this deal — the technical points where you win and the ones to avoid engaging on?"
