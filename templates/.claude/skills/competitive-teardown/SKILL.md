# /competitive-teardown

A thorough product competitive analysis — not just "what features do they have" but
what positioning they own, who they're winning against, where they're vulnerable,
and what you can do about it.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. What is your product and who is your target customer?
2. Which competitors are you analyzing? (list 2-5)
3. What is the strategic question you're trying to answer?
   - Where are we losing deals and why?
   - Should we build feature X or find a different angle?
   - Are we positioned correctly against the market?
   - Which competitor is most dangerous in the next 12 months?
4. What information do you have? (public websites / G2 reviews / trial experience / customer interviews / pricing pages)
5. What is your current differentiation claim?

Do not produce any output until the user answers all questions.

## Step 2 — Produce the Competitive Teardown

### Positioning Map

For each competitor, extract:
- **Who they say they're for** (their stated ICP from website/messaging)
- **The problem they lead with** (hero message on homepage)
- **The category they claim** (what word or phrase they use to define themselves)
- **Proof points** (customer logos, testimonials, case study themes)

Present as a positioning table + 1-paragraph narrative per competitor.

### Feature Comparison

Build a feature matrix across dimensions that matter to your target buyer:

| Capability | You | Competitor A | Competitor B | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [core feature] | ✅ / 🟡 partial / ❌ | ... | ... | [context] |

Flag the 2-3 features where gaps are most likely to lose deals.

### Pricing & Packaging

For each competitor (where public):
- Pricing model (per seat / usage / tier / custom)
- Entry price and what it includes
- The trigger that moves customers to higher tiers
- What's notably absent from their pricing (often a clue about their strategy)

### ICP Overlap

Where do your ICPs overlap and diverge?
- **Direct overlap**: same buyer, same use case — zero-sum competition
- **Partial overlap**: similar buyer, different use case — positioning battle
- **Adjacent**: different buyer or use case — not competing today but might

### Competitive Vulnerabilities

For each competitor, name the 1-2 things they cannot do without breaking what makes them valuable:

> "[Competitor A] cannot [action] without [consequence] — this is our opportunity in accounts where [condition]."

### Win/Loss Pattern

Based on available intel, what is the most common:
- **Reason you win** over each competitor?
- **Reason you lose** to each competitor?
- **Objection pattern** that appears in competitive deals?

### Strategic Recommendation

In 3-5 sentences: what should you do differently based on this analysis?
One specific action, not a list.

## Step 3 — Battle Card

Ask: "Do you want me to produce a one-page sales battle card for the most dangerous competitor — the cheat sheet your sales team uses in a competitive deal?"
