# /andreessen

Applies Marc Andreessen's market-first decision framework to any opportunity, product idea,
strategy, or career move. The core insight: in technology, market size and timing beat
team quality and product quality. The question is never "is this a good idea?" —
it's "is this the right market at the right time?"

Use this when evaluating an opportunity and conventional analysis feels insufficient.

## Step 1 — Gather Context

Use AskUserQuestion to collect:

1. What is the opportunity, decision, or idea you're evaluating? (describe it plainly)
2. What's your current leaning? (optimistic / skeptical / genuinely undecided)
3. What specifically is making you uncertain? (market size / timing / competition / execution / other)

Do not produce any output until the user answers.

## Step 2 — The Andreessen Framework Analysis

Apply the framework in this order — market factors before execution factors:

### 1. Market Size
Is this a big market, or could it become one?
- What is the total addressable market (TAM) today?
- What's the realistic serviceable market in 5 years?
- Is this a market that is growing, shrinking, or being created from scratch?

**Andreessen's rule**: A great team in a bad market loses. A mediocre team in a great market wins.
If the market isn't big enough, everything else is moot.

### 2. Market Timing
Why now, not 5 years ago or 5 years from now?
- What has changed recently that creates or unlocks this opportunity? (regulation, technology, behavior shift, cost curve)
- What would have made this impossible or impractical 3 years ago?
- What would make it too late in 3 years?

**The timing question is the hardest one.** Most failed startups were right about the market
but wrong about the timing.

### 3. Competitive Dynamics
What does the competitive landscape look like?
- Who is doing this already, and why haven't they won?
- What is the defensible position (network effects / switching costs / proprietary data / brand)?
- Is this a winner-take-all market or does it support multiple players?

### 4. Team / Execution (last, not first)
Given the market and timing analysis:
- What capabilities does winning this market specifically require?
- Does the team have those, or can they build them?

### Verdict

One of:
- **Strong opportunity** — market timing is right, size is real, path to defensibility exists
- **Too early** — the market exists but the forcing function hasn't arrived yet
- **Too late** — strong incumbents, late-mover disadvantage likely
- **Wrong market** — interesting idea but market won't support the ambition
- **Conditional** — strong IF [specific assumption holds]

In 3-4 sentences: the most important thing the framework revealed that wasn't obvious before.

## Step 3 — Stress Test

Ask: "Do you want me to argue the opposite position — the strongest case for why this is the wrong opportunity?"

If yes: steelman the counter-case. The goal is to find the assumption that, if wrong, kills everything.
Name that assumption explicitly at the end.
