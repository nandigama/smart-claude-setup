# Client Brief Template

**How to use**: Paste this into Claude with your answers filled in, or invoke `/client-brief`
in Claude Code.

---

## Tell Claude This First

```
Write a client brief using the format below.

Client: [client name and one sentence about who they are]
Their problem: [what pain or goal is driving this engagement]
Your proposed solution: [what you're delivering]
Timeline: [when it starts, key milestones, when it ends]
Budget/scope: [constraints, if shareable in the brief]
Success criteria: [how you'll both know this worked]
Audience for this brief: [who reads it — exec, procurement, project team?]
Tone: [formal / collaborative / technical]
```

---

## Output Format

**Executive Summary** (3 sentences max)
[The whole brief in three sentences: problem, solution, outcome.]

**The Challenge**
[One paragraph. The client's current situation and why it matters now.]

**Our Approach**
[2-4 bullet points covering the key elements of your solution. Jargon-free.]

**Deliverables**
[Numbered list of what they receive. Concrete, not vague.]

**Timeline**
| Milestone | Date |
|---|---|
| [Kick-off] | [Date] |
| [Mid-point] | [Date] |
| [Delivery] | [Date] |

**Next Steps**
[3 action items: who does what by when. Always the last section.]

---

## Tips

- Keep the executive summary to 3 sentences — executives stop reading after that
- Deliverables should be nouns, not verbs ("A 20-page audit report" not "Audit the system")
- Next Steps should name a person, not say "the team"
