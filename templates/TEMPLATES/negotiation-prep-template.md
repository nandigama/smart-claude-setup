# Negotiation Prep Template

**How to use**: Paste this into Claude with your answers filled in, or invoke `/negotiation-prep`
in Claude Code.

---

## Tell Claude This First

```
Help me prepare for a negotiation.

What it's about: [e.g., salary negotiation, contract renewal, vendor pricing, scope change]
My ideal outcome: [be specific — a number, a term, an agreement]
My walk-away position: [the minimum you'll accept before you walk]
Their likely goal: [what the other side wants]
My BATNA: [your best alternative if this negotiation fails]
History/context: [anything Claude needs to know about the relationship or past attempts]
Stakes: [what happens if this goes badly]
```

---

## Output I Want

**My Position** (2-3 sentences I could literally say to open)
[The opening statement. Clear, non-defensive, states what I want without apologizing for it.]

**Their Likely Position**
[What they want and the reasons behind it — their interests, not just their position.]

**Common Ground**
[Where we actually agree — start here, not with the gap.]

## Roleplay Option

After the prep package: "Roleplay the negotiation with me. You play the other side."
This is the most valuable part — do it before every high-stakes conversation.
